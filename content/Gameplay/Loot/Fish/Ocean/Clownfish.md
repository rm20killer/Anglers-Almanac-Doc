This little orange spark is a feisty one, flitting in and out of stinging anemones as if it owned the place. It has a special coating on its scales that prevents it from getting stung by its hosts—a useful trait for a fish that’s essentially a neon sign for predators. Spotting one is a good indication that a reef is nearby, and trouble won’t be far behind.
